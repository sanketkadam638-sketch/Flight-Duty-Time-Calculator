Basis & limitations: figures reflect the widely-published summary of DGCA CAR Section 7, Series J, Part I (Cabin Crew FDTL). WOCL is taken as 02:00–05:59, with a 100% reduction (capped at 2h) when a duty starts inside it and a 50% reduction when a duty ends in or spans it. Sign-off time is taken as chocks-on and is assumed to already include post-flight briefing — no separate buffer is added. The 15h FDP allowance for the >8:00–11:00 flight-time band only applies with 3 sectors or fewer; beyond 3 sectors in that band the maximum reverts to 12h. An unforeseen extension of the Duty Period (DP) beyond the adjusted max FDP is permissible at the commander's discretion up to 4 hours in any 24 hours; the extension is doubled and added to the rest owed after that duty as compensatory rest (comp-off). Note that DGCA's scheme also caps cumulative extensions at 10 hours in any 28 days — this tool checks the single-duty 4-hour cap only, so track your rolling 28-day extension total separately. The next legal reporting time accounts for the required rest plus the opted transport time both after sign-off (going home) and before the next report (returning to the airport). The flight-time/FDP/landings table is a simplified version of the regulation; DGCA's full scheme also varies FDP by number of sectors within each band in finer steps. This tool is a planning aid only — always verify against your operator's current, DGCA-approved FDTL Scheme (part of the Operations Manual), which is the sole authoritative source and may reflect a newer CAR issue.
